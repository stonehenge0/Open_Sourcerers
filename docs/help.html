Charities that work on preventing existential crises for humanity (f.e. climate change, nuclear war) are x-crisis-charities and will be shown if you select "yes".

Cost-efficiency is a way to measure how much good a charity does for a set amount of money. This is important because the amount of good charities can do with a given amount of money can be hugely different. Cost-efficiency can take into account the spendings on administrative work, how much room for funding a charity has and much more. If you want to read more about cost-efficiency, you can have a look at how one of our sources, charity evaluator GiveWell, handles cost-efficiency.
